## Buyer's Guide: Choosing Your System

### Filter Types Explained

**Reverse Osmosis (RO):** Uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove nearly all dissolved contaminants, including TDS. The right choice when you want tap water that tests close to bottled water quality.

**Under-Sink Carbon:** Handles taste and odor (chlorine primarily) at high flow rates but doesn't strip dissolved solids or minerals. Lower upfront cost and simpler maintenance.

**Countertop / Pitcher:** Portable and accessible, but requires frequent refills and provides shallower filtration. Best as an entry point or secondary system.

### What to Evaluate Before Buying

**Certifications:** NSF/ANSI 58 covers RO systems; 42 and 53 cover carbon and other reduction claims. If a filter doesn't carry NSF certification, the performance claims are unverified.

**Pure-to-Drain Ratio:** RO systems produce waste water. A 2:1 ratio is a reasonable baseline—a 1:3 ratio (one gallon clean, three wasted) adds meaningful cost over time on a metered water supply.

**Cost Per Gallon:** A filter priced at $20 that runs 40 gallons costs $0.50/gallon. A $100 filter running 2,000 gallons costs $0.05/gallon. Calculate the actual operating cost before assuming a cheaper filter is the better deal.

### Installation Considerations

Under-sink RO systems require a dedicated faucet hole and, for tankless models like the G3P800, a 110V outlet under the cabinet. Most homeowners comfortable with basic plumbing can handle the install. If you're not, budget for 30 minutes of a plumber's time—it's a straightforward job.

## FAQ

**Does a water filter remove beneficial minerals?**
RO systems remove nearly everything, including calcium and magnesium. If mineral taste matters to you, a remineralization filter inline after the membrane adds them back selectively. Most users don't notice the difference.

**How do I know when to replace a Waterdrop filter?**
The G3 and G2 series include smart indicators on the faucet display that shift from blue to red when the filter reaches capacity. No guesswork on replacement timing.

**Is a tankless system worth the premium over a tank system?**
For most households, yes. Tankless designs eliminate the storage tank where bacterial growth can occur, provide continuous supply without waiting for a tank to refill, and free up significant cabinet space. The cost premium is modest compared to the ongoing operational advantages.

**Can these systems handle well water?**
Yes, with a caveat. If your well has high sediment load or iron bacteria, install a sediment pre-filter upstream of the RO unit. Without it, particulates shorten membrane life significantly and increase replacement costs.

**Does the G3P800 require professional installation?**
The system is designed for DIY installation using push-to-connect fittings. Based on owner reports, most installs run 30-60 minutes with basic hand tools and a YouTube video running in the background.
