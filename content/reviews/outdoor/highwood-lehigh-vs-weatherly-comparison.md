## Quick Verdict

Choose Lehigh if you want a traditional New England vertical-slat profile with the widest range of product configurations — including a gliding bench, counter-height balcony set, and purpose-matched dining options. Choose Weatherly if you want a modern farmhouse aesthetic with horizontal back lines and a classic floor-contact rocking chair experience on a level porch surface.

---

## Material — Identical Across Both Collections

Both collections are manufactured in Pennsylvania from HDPE poly-lumber with 304-grade stainless steel hardware. Neither rots, cracks, or requires surface treatment. Both use the NatureTEX embossed finish, and both carry the same 20-year limited residential structural warranty. The material is not a differentiator between these two collections.

For a detailed breakdown of HDPE poly-lumber material properties, see our [Highwood Lehigh Collection Guide](/outdoor/highwood-lehigh-collection-guide/).

## Design Aesthetic — Where They Diverge

The Lehigh uses vertical back slats with rounded tops — a structured, formal silhouette that matches Colonial, Cottage, and Craftsman-style homes. It reads as traditional New England garden furniture: upright, clean-lined, suited to formal garden layouts and covered porches where a polished appearance is the goal.

The Weatherly uses horizontal back slats for a bolder, more relaxed profile. The aesthetic fits Modern Farmhouse and traditional Southern porch styles — a homestead feel with slightly more contemporary lines. Where the Lehigh sits formal, the Weatherly sits casual.

Both share the same color palette and NatureTEX finish, which means pieces from each collection can be mixed in the same space without clashing. If you have a Lehigh bench on one side of a porch and add a Weatherly rocking chair later, the material and finish are consistent enough to read as intentional rather than mismatched.

## The Weatherly Rocking Chair — The Weatherly Anchor Product

The Weatherly Rocking Chair weighs approximately 33 lbs and carries a 400 to 500 lb weight capacity depending on production run — verify the current spec on the product page. The construction is the same HDPE poly-lumber and 304-grade stainless hardware as the Lehigh collection.

The motion mechanism is the relevant technical distinction from the Lehigh Gliding Bench. The Weatherly uses curved runners in direct contact with the floor. That floor contact creates the traditional pitch-and-return rocking motion — a different physical feel from the Lehigh glider's horizontal parallel travel. The trade-off is surface requirement: curved runners need a flat, level surface to rock evenly. On irregular pavers, stamped concrete with texture variation, or uneven deck boards, the chair can walk or rock unevenly. The Lehigh Gliding Bench's internal frame mechanism handles those surfaces without issue because the base stays stationary.

For a covered porch with a smooth, level floor, the Weatherly delivers the classic rocking chair experience. For any surface with variation, the Lehigh glider is the more practical motion-seating option.

## Making the Call

Choose Lehigh if you want the full range of configuration options — gliding bench, balcony set, conversation set with matched side table — or if the vertical slat New England aesthetic matches your home's exterior style.

Choose Weatherly if the horizontal back profile is a better fit for a Modern Farmhouse or Southern porch aesthetic, and your primary use case is a classic rocking chair on a flat, level surface.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between Highwood Lehigh and Weatherly?**
The difference is design aesthetic and product range. Lehigh has vertical back slats and a formal New England silhouette with the widest product selection including the gliding bench and balcony set. Weatherly has horizontal back slats and a more relaxed farmhouse profile, centered on rocking chair seating. Both collections use identical HDPE poly-lumber and hardware.

**Do Highwood collections mix and match?**
Yes. Both collections share the same color options and NatureTEX finish. Lehigh and Weatherly pieces in matching colors read as intentional in the same space — the material and finish consistency is close enough that mixing is a practical option for buyers who want the Weatherly rocking chair alongside Lehigh benches or chairs.

**Is Highwood furniture made in the USA?**
Yes. Both the Lehigh and Weatherly collections are manufactured in Pennsylvania using recycled HDPE and 304-grade stainless steel hardware.
