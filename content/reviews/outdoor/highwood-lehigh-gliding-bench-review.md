## The Gliding Mechanism — How It Works

The bench uses a parallel-plane gliding system. The base frame sits stationary on the floor. Four heavy-duty swing arms connect the seating surface to that frame, allowing the seat to travel forward and back while remaining horizontal throughout the range of motion.

The practical difference from a rocking bench: a rocker travels across the floor during use, requiring a smooth, level surface and a specific clearance zone behind the piece. The glider's motion is entirely internal to the frame — the base doesn't move. This makes it functional on pavers, stamped concrete, or slightly uneven deck boards where a rocker would walk or become unstable.

The pivot points at the swing arm connections are the primary mechanical wear items in any glider. Highwood uses stainless steel hardware at those junctions, which prevents the oxidation-driven seizure and squeaking that develops in cheaper galvanized or standard steel hardware after a few seasons of outdoor exposure. Based on aggregated owner reports, the glide mechanism remains smooth over multiple seasons without lubrication or adjustment.

Allow 6 to 8 inches of clearance in front of and behind the unit for full travel — the seat needs that space to complete its range of motion without contacting a railing or table.

## Poly-Lumber Material Analysis

HDPE poly-lumber has no cellular structure, which means no moisture absorption pathway. The bench won't rot, won't support mold or fungal growth, and won't crack from freeze-thaw cycles. These aren't features — they're the absence of failure modes that are built into organic wood.

Color is integral to the material, compounded in during extrusion rather than applied as a surface coat. UV stabilizers are built in the same way. A scratch reveals identical color underneath, and there's no coating to chip, peel, or separate from the substrate. Aggregated owner feedback on Highwood's site consistently notes that the NatureTEX embossed surface reduces the flat-plastic visual quality of lower-grade recycled products.

The maintenance comparison over five years is concrete: a teak bench requires approximately 10 to 15 hours of labor and over $100 in specialized oils across that period to maintain structural integrity and color. The Lehigh bench requires a wash with mild soap, occasionally. Cedar is lower cost to maintain than teak but still requires bi-annual sealing to prevent graying and structural degradation. Poly-lumber has no equivalent cost or time requirement in that window.

## Where Buyers Run Into Problems

Four areas come up consistently in owner feedback and are worth knowing before purchase.

Weight is the first. At 71 lbs, this bench doesn't move easily by one person. If you need furniture that relocates frequently or gets carried for seasonal storage, this is the wrong piece. For a permanent installation that stays in one place for years, the weight is a functional feature — it doesn't blow around.

Cushion compatibility is the second. The exterior frame depth is 28 inches, but actual seat depth is less. Standard big-box bench cushions sized to 28 inches may fit loosely or leave a gap at the back. Verify actual seat dimensions on the product page before ordering third-party cushions.

Assembly is the third. HDPE doesn't flex like wood, which means pre-drilled hole alignment requires precision. Owner reports indicate 45 to 90 minutes for assembly. A power drill with a hex bit set to low torque speeds the process; the included manual tools work but are fatiguing across the number of fasteners involved. A second person is useful for holding the heavy frame sections in alignment during assembly.

Price is the fourth. At approximately $800, the upfront cost is significantly higher than a pressure-treated wood bench at $150 to $200. The annualized cost argument holds over a 20-year horizon — three or four wooden bench replacement cycles plus maintenance cost exceeds the Lehigh price in most climates. But the capital outlay at purchase is real and requires planning for.

## Making the Call

Buy the Lehigh Gliding Bench if you want permanent motion seating in a coastal or high-humidity environment where wood fails quickly, your porch lacks the ceiling structure for a hanging swing, and you're making a long-term investment in a piece that won't need maintenance attention for 20 years.

Skip it if you're budget-constrained and a lower-cost wooden bench that you'll maintain yourself is a workable plan, if you specifically value the texture and warmth of natural wood grain, or if you need furniture one person can move without effort.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does the Highwood Lehigh gliding bench last?**
Highwood's 20-year limited residential warranty covers structural defects. HDPE itself doesn't biodegrade, so the material will outlast the warranty period under normal conditions. The practical service life limit is more likely hardware fatigue at the pivot points than material failure — stainless steel hardware significantly extends that timeline versus standard steel.

**Does the Highwood gliding bench need cushions?**
Most owner reports describe the contoured seat as comfortable without cushions. If you add them, use deep-seating bench cushions and verify the actual seat depth on the product page before ordering — the 28-inch exterior frame depth and the functional seat depth differ.

**How hard is the Lehigh bench to assemble?**
Straightforward but time-consuming due to fastener count and the density of the material. Expect 45 to 90 minutes. A second person is useful for holding heavy frame sections during alignment. A power drill with a hex bit on low torque is faster than the included manual tools.

**Is the Highwood gliding bench worth the price?**
For permanent outdoor installation in climates with humidity, salt air, or significant seasonal variation, the annualized cost over 20 years is lower than the recurring maintenance and replacement cycle for wood alternatives. For buyers in dry climates who are comfortable with annual wood maintenance, the price premium is harder to justify.
